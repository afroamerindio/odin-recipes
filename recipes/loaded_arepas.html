Loaded Arepas

Arepas! Have you had them? Let’s talk about what they are. These are a really big deal in my life right now.

These gems are South American pockets of fried cornmeal-like dough (harina precocida if you want to get technical) and they’re stuffed with every possible good thing you could ever want stuffed in a pocket of fried cornmeal-like dough. You most commonly find them being made in Colombia, Venezuela, Bolivia, and Panama.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups precooked cornmeal*
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 1/2 cups warm water
  • Oil for pan frying (you only need a tiny ammount)

Filling ideas

Arepas are very vesatile when it comes to what to fill them with:

  • Fried eggs and Avocados
  • Carnitas (personal fave!) or Chipotle Shredded Chicken
  • Magic Green Sauce or other sauce (umm, what other sauce would there be?)
  • Black beans
  • sweet potatoes, sliced into thin pieces and sautéed in olive oil and salt until softened
  • red onions (pickled? yum)
  • Cotija cheese
  • Or all of the above

Preparation

The procces of making arepas slightly changes from country to country and region to region. Below is the most common method:

  1. Combine your ingredients: Mix the precooked cornmeal and the salt. Add the water (warm water) and whisk remove any lumps, then stir until combined. Use your hands mix and get a consistent dough. Let the mixture rest for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Form the arepas: Using your hands, divide the dough into 8 pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and flatten in gently to create a disk, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Fry the arepas(?): Heat a thin layer of oil (oil is only needed to stop the arepas stiking to the pan) in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. Add the arepas and fry for about 6 minutes on both sides. The arepas should get a dry fried exterior without getting overly brown. Set on paper towels to drain and cool.
  4. Assemble: Cut the arepas in half and stuff with your fillings!
